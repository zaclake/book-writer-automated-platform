## Consistency & Redundancy Gate

**Purpose**: Prevent POV/name drift, stitched outputs, and repeated scene functions across all genres.

---

## Identity Consistency Rules

- **Single protagonist name** per chapter (no aliases unless introduced intentionally).
- **Single POV mode** per chapter unless explicitly designed for multi-POV.
- **Avatar names** must remain stable once established.

**Failure Triggers:**
- Protagonist name changes without narrative justification.
- POV slips from first to third or between characters without transition.
- Identity references conflict (e.g., Player/Aegis/Alex in same chapter).

---

## Beat-Function Deduplication

Every scene must have a **primary function** (setup, escalation, reveal, reversal, payoff).

Rules:
- **No duplicate scene functions** in a single chapter unless the second instance escalates stakes or changes the information set.
- **No duplicate onboarding beats** (tutorials, awe, first quest, first combat) without a major new dimension.
- **No recap-only scenes** (recaps must be embedded in active scenes).

---

## Repetition Limits

- **Recap allowance**: max 3 sentences per chapter.
- **Onboarding beats**: max 1 per chapter.
- **First-encounter beats**: max 1 per chapter unless a new faction or system is introduced.

---

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Protagonist name and identity are consistent across the chapter.
- [ ] POV is stable with intentional transitions only.
- [ ] Scene functions are unique or escalated.
- [ ] No redundant onboarding beats.
- [ ] Recap content is minimal and embedded in active scenes.
