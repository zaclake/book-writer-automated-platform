## Distinctiveness & Specificity Tests

**Purpose**: Eliminate generic prose and ensure each scene contains proprietary world texture.

---

## Unique World Marker Rule

Every scene must include **at least one** proprietary detail that is unique to the story world:
- Economy or currency behavior
- Social/cultural rule specific to the setting
- Technology/magic rule with constraints
- Branded system, organization, or ritual
- Environmental or sensory feature that exists only in this world

Failure Trigger: scene contains only stock genre elements without a unique marker.

---

## Specificity Requirements

Per paragraph:
- **Minimum 2 concrete nouns** (objects, locations, tools, materials).
- **One sensory anchor** (sound, texture, smell, temperature, visual detail).

Per scene:
- **At least one** proprietary world marker.
- **One** detail that implies rules or constraints in the world.

---

## Generic Lexicon Penalty

Flag the scene if:
- Stock terms (e.g., “orc,” “wizard,” “quest board,” “HUD”) appear **without** a unique modifier or system rule tied to this world.
- Descriptions could be copied into any other book of the same genre.

---

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Scene includes a unique world marker.
- [ ] Each paragraph contains 2+ concrete nouns.
- [ ] Each paragraph includes a sensory anchor.
- [ ] At least one detail implies a world rule or constraint.
- [ ] No stock genre terms appear without unique context.
