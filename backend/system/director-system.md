## Director System (First-Draft Guidance Layer)

**Purpose**: Produce world-class first drafts by translating story inputs into concrete, flexible scene direction.  
The director system does not replace authorial voice. It sets intent, payload, and guardrails so the LLM can write naturally.

---

## Core Inputs (All Must Be Used)

1. **Book Bible** (story promise, tone, voice, arc, non-negotiables)
2. **Reference Files** (characters, outline, world-building, style guide, timeline, themes, research)
3. **Director Guide** (project-wide guidance for natural prose)
4. **Vector Memory** (continuity-critical facts and recent context)
5. **Chapter Plan** (objectives, required plot points, POV, opening/ending types)
6. **Continuity Snapshot** (ledger, unresolved questions, bridge requirements)
7. **Global Arc Diagnostics** (progress, pacing trend, plot-thread status, theme balance)

---

## Director Brief (Per Chapter)

The director produces a brief before any first-draft writing. It must be concise and concrete.

Required sections:
- **Chapter Intent**: Why this chapter exists in the arc.
- **Opening Onramp Strategy**: How to ease in without stalling (especially Chapter 1).
- **Scene Cards**: 2–4 scenes with:
  - Scene Function
  - Goal
  - Obstacle
  - Choice
  - Result
  - Consequence (cost/gain/obligation)
  - Unique World Marker
  - Emotional Beat to Show
- **Freedom Levers**: What the writer can improvise.
- **Avoid/Do-Not-Repeat**: Redundant beats, phrases, images.
- **Ending Hook**: Specific unresolved obligation.

---

## Naturalness & LLM Freedom

The director brief is **not** a script. It is intent plus payload.  
LLMs should be instructed to:
- Use the brief as guidance, not a checklist.
- Favor organic phrasing and scene-level truth.
- Vary sentence rhythm and avoid mechanical patterns.
- Choose the most natural expression that satisfies the intent.

---

## Opening Onramp Standard

Chapter 1 should **ease in** without delay:
- Anchor in daily life or routine (1 concrete, specific detail).
- Reveal character wound or need through behavior.
- Signal story promise and stakes.
- End with a clear obligation that pulls the reader forward.

---

## Output Quality Indicators

- Every scene changes the story state.
- No redundant onboarding beats.
- Each scene carries at least one unique world marker.
- The chapter ends with a specific obligation.
- Prose reads like a human author, not a checklist.
